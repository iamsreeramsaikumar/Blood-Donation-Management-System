A+

“ A + is the second most common blood type ”
  • Donors are encouraged to give whole blood, platelets and plasma.
    • FROM A TO AB
  • A+ can give red blood cells to other A+ and AB+ recipients.
    • A POSITIVE RECEIVER
  • Those with A+ can only receive red blood cells from A+, A-, O+, O- blood types.
    • POPULAR POSITIVITY
  • A+ is found in 34% of people, making it the second most common blood type behind O+.

    • A-

    “ A- is a powerful blood type ”
  • Donors are encouraged to give red blood cells, whole blood or platelets.
    • FROM A TO AB
  • A- can give red blood cells to other A- as well as A+,A-, AB+ and AB- recipients.
    • THE RIGHT TYPE OF GIVER
  • Those with A- can only receive red blood cells from A- or O- blood types.
    • A SPECIAL DONOR
  • A- is only found in 6% of people, so donations are always needed.

    • B+

    “ B+ is a community supporter ”
  • Donors are encouraged to give red blood cells, whole blood or platelets.
    • LIFESAVING POSITIVES
  • B+can give red blood cells to other B+ and AB+ recipients.
    • POSITIVE RECEIVER
  • Those with B+ can only receive red blood cells from B+,B-, O+.O- blood types.
    • THE POWER OF POSITIVITY
  • Since various types of B+ donations are useful, donations are important. Only 8% of the population has B+ blood .

    • B-

    “ B- is an uncommon blood type ”
  • Donors are encouraged to give red blood cells, whole blood or platelets.
    • EVERYTHING FROM A TO B
  • B- can give red blood cells to other B+,B-,AB+ and AB-.
    • THE RIGHT TYPE OF GIVER
  • Those with B- can only receive red blood cells from B- or O- blood types.
    • A VITAL BLOOD TYPE
  • B- is only found in less than 2% of the population, making it an incredibly important blood type to have on the shelves.

    • AB+

    “ AB+ is the universal recipient ”
  • Donors are encouraged to give platelets and plasma, although whole blood donations are always welcome.
    • FROM ONE TO ANOTHER
  • AB+ can only give red blood cells to other AB+ blood types.
    • THE UNIVERSAL RECEIVER
  • Those with AB+ can receive red blood cells from all blood types. Type AB donors can donate plasma and platelets to all blood types.
    • THE POWER OF POSITIVITY
  • AB+ is only found in 3% of the population, so donations are incredibly important for patients in need of plasma.

    • AB-

    “ AB- is the rarest blood type ”
  • Donors are encouraged to give platelets and/or plasma, but whole blood donations are always welcome.
    • FROM ONE AB TO ANOTHER; UNIVERSAL PLASMA DONOR
  • AB- can give red blood cells to both AB- and AB+ blood types. AB- can give platelets or plasma to a patient of any blood type.
    • MAKING NEGATIVE A POSITIVE
  • Those with AB- can receive red blood cells from all negative blood types.
    • DONATIONS ARE VITAL
  • AB- is only found in less than 1% of the population, making it the most rare and highly in-demand blood type. AB is the most desirable blood type for platelet and plasma donations.

    • O+

    “ O+ is the most common blood type ”
  • Donors are encouraged to give red blood cells and whole blood donations.
    • THE POWER OF POSITIVITY
  • O+ can give red blood cells to other Rh positive blood types.
    • POSITIVES AND NEGATIVES
  • Those with O+ can only receive red blood cells from O+ or O- donors.
    • POPULAR POSITIVITY
  • O+ is found in 38% of people, making it the most common blood type.

    • O-

    “ O- is the highest in-demand blood type ”
  • Donors are encouraged to give red cells and whole blood donations.
    • GIVE NOT TAKERS
  • Those with O- can only receive from other O- donors.
    • THE UNIVERSAL DONOR
  • O- blood type is the universal red blood cell donor because their red blood cells can be transfused into any patient, regardless of blood type. O- red cells are used for trauma situations and other emergencies when the patient’s blood type is not known. O- red cells are also frequently used for babies and sickle cell anemia patients.
    • ALWAYS IN DEMAND
  • O- makes up only 6.6% of the population, found in just 1 of every 15 people.
